A Resistance: A story of Yu Gwan-soon

Aunae market

* This is where the independence movement took place on April 1, 1919.
Yu Gwan-soon was only 17 years old when the independence movement took place on March 1, 1919. Yu Gwan-soon joined the movement over the school wall with his friends and shouted "Mansae" with tens of thousands of students and citizens. After that, she went back to her hometown(Cheonan) and agreed with the village elders to hold the independence Movement, and they planned to hold the Movement at Aunae Market on April 1st when would be March 1st in the lunar calendar.
She actively led the independence movement by distributing the national flag to the crowd. Many people were injured and killed in the independence movement on this day. After the movement, Yu Gwan-soon was sentenced to three years in prison and jailed in Seodaemun Prison.

Seodaemun Prison History Hall

As you can see in the movie, Yu Gwan-soon, who was imprisoned in Seodaemun Prison, was severely tortured for shouting "Independence hurrah" in prison. After a brief pause due to the dissuasion of Park In-deok, a teacher at Ewha School who had been imprisoned together, Yu Gwan-soon led the independence movement in prison again on the March 1, 1920 that was the first anniversary of the movement. At that time, the her bladder ruptured under torture, and eventually Yu Gwan-soon died in Seodaemun Prison on September 28, 1920.
