Spirits's Homecoming

House of Sharing

Movie "Spirits' Homecoming" was released in February, 2016. Director Jo Jungrae to this film did volunteer work for the Japanese military sex slavery in 2002 for the first time and he decided to make a film after seeing ‘Burned Her Companions’, a picture of Gang ilchun. He wanted to express the cruelty of Japanese military and life of their sex slavery. ‘The House of Sharing’ located in Gwangju, Gyeonggi Province. It is using as a place that old Japanese military sexual victims living together, and let people know the truth about Japanese military sex slavery crimes against humanity as a historical museum. Japanese military sex slavery were women and girls forced into being sex slaves by the Imperial Japanese Army in occupied countries and territories during Japanese colonial era. This movie was made in the difficult surroundings. The movie's staff filmed the movie at their own expense, and in the case of actors, they appeared in the movie as no guarantee. Despite such efforts, however, there were too few theaters for the film in Korea. In particular, the actors who played in the film were threatened by various groups.

Statue of Peace

It is symbol that represents victims of Japanese military sex slavery problem. It was first set up in front of the Japanese Embassy in Seoul on December 14, 2011.
There is an empty chair next to the statue, which means to empathize with their suffering.
