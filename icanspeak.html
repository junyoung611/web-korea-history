I Can Speak

United States Capitol

The movie "I can speak" is based on the story of Lee Yong-soo and Kim Gun-ja. They stated related to a Japanese military sex slavery by attending the hearing in the United States. They did not speak in English as in the movie, but they gave moving testimony in Korean, contributing to the adoption of the resolution by the U.S. House of Representatives.
The resolution is "HR121," which calls for Japan to apologize for waging an invasion war and committing anti-humanitarian acts. Since Japan has never made an official apology before, this is a historically important resolution. The resolution was officially adopted by the U.S. House Foreign Affairs Committee on June 26, 2007 with 39 votes in favor and two in opposition, and was subsequently unanimously passed. This means the international community officially admitted for the first time that the Japanese forced mobilization Japanese military sex slaves.
